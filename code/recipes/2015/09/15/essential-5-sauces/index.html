My Essential 5 Hot Sauces

Sep 15, 2015

Hot sauce runs through my veins. Also beer and coffee, but those are topics for another day. As my intoductory post on opensa.us, I’ll keep it simple and stick to my essentials for keeping my food saucy. The first on the list is an outstanding homemade sauce, and the rest are store bought:

1. Homemade Romesco Sauce

This one is so damn good and totally worth the time. The right balance of smoky, nutty, spicy makes this sauce a mouth watering addition to burgers, stuffed peppers, as a dip for mezza, and I even added some to make lentil soup smoky and spicy.

  • 1 cup Los Roast’s roasted red chili peppers (local Portland brand, but can be substituted)
  • 1 1/2 tbs pimentón
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup blanched almonds (slow roasted in oil for best results)
  • 2 tbsps Sherry Vinegar (don’t get really crappy sherry vinegar, there can be a big difference in quality)
  • (optional) some additional spice if desired, such as the aardavark sauce or habenero sauce below
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tbs olive oil, 4 tbs safflower oil

Add all ingredients except olive oil, and egg yolk. Puree in a food processor for 30 seconds, then continue to puree as you add egg yolk and drizzle olive oil on top.

2. Aardavark Sauce - Hot Habenero

A Portland staple. If you haven’t yet tried this sauce, then you’re truly missing out. It has the spice that you expect, but doesn’t have as much opinion as other spices. Unlike most other sauces, you can pair it with basically everything: burgers, stir frys, mediterranean, pastas, or most everything except ice cream (but I probably shouldn’t say that without first trying it).

If you aren’t local, then you can get this awesome sauce online.

3. Trader Joe’s Habenero Hot sauce

This sauce is very hot, but just a few drops gives that warm slow burn that makes habenero’s my favorite pepper. Add a couple drops when cooking and the heat will desperse throughout the whole dish. There are plenty of other great habenero sauce options, but TJ’s cheap little bottle will stand up next to the best of them.

4. Huy Fong’s Rooster Sriracha

If you’re a trader joe’s shopper, I caution you to stay away from their sriracha. Stick with the classic. This one won’t let you down.

5. Franks Red Hot Original

The sauce that I grew up on, and often still find myself “putting that s*** on everything.”
